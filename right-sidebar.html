Research

Highlighted Research Work

As part of my B.Sc. in Electrical and Electronic Engineering at Ahsanullah University of Science and Technology (AUST), I conducted research on the design of power electronic converters for hybrid renewable energy and electric vehicle applications.

Design of Power Electronic Converters

My undergraduate thesis focused on designing and implementing DC-AC and DC-DC converters optimized for hybrid renewable energy systems and electric vehicles. The converters were optimized for low Total Harmonic Distortion (THD) and high efficiency, achieving over 98% efficiency and around 1.17% THD for current and 2.5% for voltage.

Technical Contributions

The research included the development of an Enhanced Gain Buck-Boost Converter (EGBBC) and an inverter with a double-layer sandwiched L-C filter. These designs operated across four modes, significantly improving voltage gain, efficiency, and reducing THD.

Tools and Techniques

This project was conducted using advanced simulation tools such as MATLAB and PSIM, focusing on optimizing parameters like inductance, capacitance, and frequency to evaluate and enhance output voltage performance.

This research is a crucial step towards advancing more efficient and reliable power electronics, which is pivotal for the growth of renewable energy and electric vehicle technologies.

Thesis Overview

The thesis titled "Design of Power Electronic Converters with Enhanced Performance Parameters for Hybrid Renewable Energy and Electric Vehicle Applications" focused on innovative power electronic designs to enhance the efficiency and reliability of renewable energy systems and electric vehicles.
